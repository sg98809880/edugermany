Path to German PR

From student visa to
permanent residence

1

Student Visa

Arrive on a German student visa (Visum zu Studienzwecken). Valid for the duration of your course. You can work up to 120 full days or 240 half days per year during your studies.

Duration of study
2

Job Seeker Visa (18 months)

After graduation, you can apply for an 18-month job seeker visa (Aufenthaltserlaubnis zur Arbeitssuche) to find a job in Germany related to your degree.

Up to 18 months post-graduation
3

Work Permit / EU Blue Card

Once employed, convert to a work permit or EU Blue Card. The Blue Card requires a salary threshold (€45,300+ for most fields; €41,041+ for shortage occupations like IT and engineering in 2025).

After job offer
4

Permanent Residence (Niederlassungserlaubnis)

Apply for permanent residence after 2 years of work with a Blue Card (or 3 years with a standard work permit), provided you meet language (B1) and financial requirements.

After 2 years with Blue Card
5

German Citizenship

After 5 years of legal residence (reduced to 3 years for exceptional integration), you may be eligible to apply for German citizenship and an EU passport.

After 5 years of residence

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything Indian students ask about studying in Germany

Many German universities accept IELTS as proof of English proficiency. Some universities also accept TOEFL, Cambridge certificates, or proof of prior English-medium education. Some universities offer IELTS-free admission if your bachelor's degree was taught in English. Requirements vary by program — our counsellors advise based on your specific target universities.

Yes, several German universities admit students without IELTS if your bachelor's degree was completed in English (e.g. from an Indian university with English medium of instruction). A Medium of Instruction (MOI) certificate from your university may be accepted instead. Our counsellors can identify which universities accept this for your profile.

Most German universities require a minimum CGPA of 6.5–7.0 out of 10 (approximately 60–65%). Top universities like TU Munich (TUM) or LMU Munich generally expect 7.5+ CGPA. However, a strong Statement of Purpose, relevant work experience, and research background can compensate for borderline grades at many universities.

APS (Akademische Prüfstelle) is a document verification body that checks the authenticity of academic certificates from Indian students applying to German universities. It is mandatory for most Indian applicants and involves submitting your documents and attending an in-person interview at APS India offices in New Delhi or Chennai. The process typically takes 4–8 weeks, so start early.

Yes. A large number of Master's programs — particularly in engineering, computer science, data science, and business — are taught entirely in English. German language knowledge is more important for Bachelor's programs, medical studies, and day-to-day life. We recommend learning at least A1–A2 German for practical purposes even if your program is in English.

Yes. B.Com graduates can apply for Master's programs in Business Administration (MBA/MSc), Finance, Economics, International Business, or Management at German universities. Some programs may require work experience or GMAT/GRE scores. EduGermany has helped several commerce graduates secure admissions to reputed German business schools.

Yes. After completing your degree and working for 2 years with a Blue Card (or 3 years with a regular work permit), you can apply for a German Permanent Residence Permit (Niederlassungserlaubnis). You also need to demonstrate B1-level German language proficiency and financial self-sufficiency. After 5 years of legal residence, you may be eligible for German citizenship.

Germany is one of the strongest job markets for IT professionals in Europe. Cities like Berlin, Munich, Hamburg, and Frankfurt have thriving tech scenes. Germany faces a significant shortage of skilled IT workers, and international graduates are actively sought. Average starting salaries for software engineers range from €48,000 to €70,000 per year.

The required amount in a German blocked account changes periodically based on German government regulations (it is tied to the BAföG rate). Our counsellors provide the most current figure at the time of your application and guide you through setting up the account with approved providers such as Fintiba, Coracle, or Deutsche Bank.

Depending on your visa category and financial situation, your spouse may be eligible to join you in Germany on a family reunification (dependent) visa. They may also be eligible to work in Germany. Our counsellors advise on the specific documentation and requirements for spousal visas alongside student applications.

Most German public universities (state-funded universities) charge no tuition fees for both domestic and international students, including Indian students. A semester contribution (Semesterbeitrag) of approximately €300–€700 per semester applies, which often includes a public transport pass. Some private universities and Baden-Württemberg state universities charge tuition for non-EU students.

Processing times vary by German consulate location and application volume. Typically, it takes 6–12 weeks from application submission to decision. The consulates in Chennai and Mumbai are generally faster than New Delhi. We recommend beginning your visa application process at least 3–4 months before your intended travel date.

Yes. International students in Germany can work up to 120 full days or 240 half days per year. This is generally sufficient for part-time campus jobs, student assistant (HiWi) positions, or internships. Working more than this limit requires approval from the employment agency (Agentur für Arbeit).

The EU Blue Card is a work and residence permit for highly qualified non-EU professionals. To qualify, you need a recognised university degree and a job offer that meets the minimum salary threshold (€45,300 for most professions; €41,041 for shortage occupations like IT and engineering as of 2025). It offers a faster path to permanent residence — just 2 years compared to 3 with a standard work permit.

B.Tech and B.E. graduates from India are among the most commonly admitted students to German Master's programs. Top choices include MSc in Computer Science, Electrical Engineering, Mechanical Engineering, Automotive Engineering, Data Science, Mechatronics, and Information Technology. Many programs at TU Munich, RWTH Aachen, TU Berlin, and KIT Karlsruhe are available in English.
